Robert O'Connor

Creator, Curator, Discoverer, Explorer, Doer, Multi-er

About

I've always been interested in the world, and have fulfilled this interest by participating in this wild and crazy place as best I can. My first attempt was as a musician, learning classical piano with the intention of teaching the world about lesser-known composers of the instrument. I played at the Schlern Music Festival in Italy and the Aspen Music Festival in Colorado. As time went on, my interest in India and South Asia started taking over, as did a love of jazz, so I went to Roosevelt University in Chicago with the intention of writing for their newspaper and developing as a musician. In the end, I took a degree in journalism and took internships at newspapers and magazines in India and Ireland

The more I did journalism, the more I was drawn to history, so I started writing more and more about that. My interest in the world stayed with me and I took every opportunity to see more of it. Eventually I found my way to Odyssey Publications, where I was able to induldge my various obsessions. It all culminated in writing their book Hiroshima, Nagasaki: An Illustrated History, Anthology, and Guide.

Oh, and in the meantime I did various roles at radio stations in Minneapolis and Chicago, co-produced a short-run variety show and sold subscriptions for the Chicago Symphony Orchestra (yes I was that asshole that called in the middle of dinner).

Every once in a while people ask me what I do, and the best I can describe it is I totally immerse myself in a subject, especially if it's something I know very little about and come out making things with it, and I'd like to think I know it as well as a native or expert, but that's their call.
